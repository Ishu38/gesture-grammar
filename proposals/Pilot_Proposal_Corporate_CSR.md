## The Problem

**15 million children in India live with disabilities** (Census 2011). Among them, children with cerebral palsy, hearing impairment, and non-verbal autism face a compounding disadvantage: they cannot access English language education through conventional methods that require writing, typing, or speaking.

- India has fewer than **1 speech therapist per 2.5 lakh people**
- AAC devices cost Rs. 50,000+ and teach vocabulary, not grammar
- Special schools lack structured English curriculum adapted for motor disabilities
- Result: these children grow up without the language skills needed for employment, digital access, or independent communication

**This is a solvable problem.**

## The Solution

**MLAF** (Multimodal Language Acquisition Framework) is an AI-powered application that teaches English sentence structure through hand gestures, using only a phone or tablet camera.

**A child with cerebral palsy holds up her hand. The camera sees it. The AI recognizes her gesture as the word "She." She makes another gesture — "wants." Another — "water." The screen shows: She wants water. The device speaks the sentence aloud. She just communicated a complete, grammatically correct English sentence — for the first time.**

### What makes MLAF different from existing tools:

| Feature | Typical AAC Apps | MLAF |
|---------|-----------------|------|
| Teaches grammar (sentence structure) | No | Yes — formal parser enforces SVO order, verb agreement |
| Adapts to motor ability | No | Yes — calibrates to each child's tremor and range of motion |
| Requires special hardware | Often | No — any device with a camera |
| Works offline | Rarely | Yes — all AI runs on-device |
| Cost per child | Rs. 50,000+ | Rs. 0 (software) + existing device |
| Speech output | Some | Yes — Text-to-Speech speaks constructed sentences |

### Technical credibility:
- 1D CNN gesture classifier: **99.5% accuracy** across 19 gestures
- Bayesian fusion of 3 sensory modalities (vision, audio, eye-gaze)
- Formal Chomskyan grammar engine (Earley parser) — not keyword matching
- Cognitive load adaptation — system becomes more forgiving when the child is fatigued
- **Provisional patent filed** on the core architecture

## The Pilot

We propose a **20-school pilot across 2 states**, funded under your CSR programme.

### Design

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Schools** | 20 special schools / inclusive education centres |
| **Children** | 200 (ages 6-14) with CP, hearing impairment, or non-verbal autism |
| **Duration** | 12 weeks per school |
| **Sessions** | 3 per week, 20 minutes each |
| **Devices** | 1 tablet per 2 children (shared across sessions) |
| **Staffing** | 1 trained facilitator per 5 schools (remote + in-person) |
| **Measurement** | Pre/post sentence construction test, session completion rates, teacher feedback |

### What success looks like:
- Measurable improvement in English sentence ordering ability
- Children independently constructing 3-5 word grammatical sentences by week 8
- Facilitator and teacher endorsement for continued use
- Publication-ready data for scaling to state-level adoption
